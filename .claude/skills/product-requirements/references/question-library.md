# Comprehensive Question Library

Organized question bank for product requirements gathering conversations.

## Product Vision & Purpose

### Core Problem
- What problem are you trying to solve?
- Who experiences this problem?
- How do they currently deal with this problem?
- What makes this problem worth solving?
- How painful is this problem on a scale of 1-10?

### Product Goals
- What is the primary goal of this product?
- What would make this product successful in your eyes?
- What inspired you to create this product?
- What change do you want to see in the world because of this product?
- If you could only accomplish one thing with this product, what would it be?

### Value Proposition
- Why would someone choose your product over alternatives?
- What makes your product unique or different?
- What is the one sentence description of your product?
- How would users describe your product to a friend?
- What is the "aha moment" when users realize the value?

## Target Users & Audience

### User Identification
- Who is the primary user of this product?
- Are there different types of users with different needs?
- What is the typical profile of your user (age, profession, location)?
- How tech-savvy are your users?
- What devices do they primarily use?

### User Context
- Where will users be when they use this product?
- When during their day/week will they use it?
- What are they trying to accomplish when they use it?
- What is their emotional state when using it?
- Are they using it alone or with others?

### User Needs & Pain Points
- What frustrates users about current solutions?
- What do users wish they could do but can't?
- What takes too much time in their current workflow?
- What mistakes do users commonly make?
- What causes users to give up or abandon tasks?

### User Motivations
- What motivates users to seek out this product?
- What would make them excited to use it?
- What would make them recommend it to others?
- What would make them pay for it?
- What would make them use it daily?

## Features & Functionality

### Core Features
- What are the 3 most important things users need to do?
- What is the minimum feature set for launch?
- What features are essential vs. nice-to-have?
- What features would you cut if you had to?
- What features differentiate you from competitors?

### Feature Prioritization
- Which features will users use most frequently?
- Which features provide the most value?
- Which features are easiest to build?
- Which features are most requested?
- Which features align with your core vision?

### Feature Details
- How should [specific feature] work?
- What options or settings should users have?
- What happens if [edge case]?
- Should this be automatic or user-controlled?
- What information does the user need to see?

### Feature Scope
- What should this feature NOT do?
- Where do you draw the line on complexity?
- What related features are out of scope?
- What can be added later vs. needed now?
- What features might confuse or overwhelm users?

## User Experience & Workflows

### User Journey
- How does a user first discover your product?
- What happens when they first open/visit it?
- What is the first action they take?
- What is the typical sequence of actions?
- How does a session typically end?

### Onboarding
- What does a new user need to know immediately?
- How much guidance do users need?
- What information do you need from users upfront?
- How do you demonstrate value quickly?
- What might cause users to leave during onboarding?

### Core Workflows
- Walk me through a typical use case from start to finish.
- What are the steps to accomplish [main task]?
- What decisions does the user need to make?
- What information does the user need at each step?
- Where might users get stuck or confused?

### Interaction Patterns
- How do users navigate between sections?
- How do users create/edit/delete items?
- How do users search or filter?
- How do users save or share their work?
- What actions should be quick vs. deliberate?

### Error Handling
- What happens if something goes wrong?
- How should errors be communicated?
- Can users undo mistakes?
- What happens if users lose connection?
- How do you prevent users from making mistakes?

## Content & Data

### Data Input
- What information do users need to provide?
- How much data entry is required?
- Can data be imported from elsewhere?
- What formats should be supported?
- How do you validate user input?

### Data Display
- What information needs to be visible at a glance?
- How should data be organized?
- What level of detail is appropriate?
- How should large amounts of data be handled?
- What visualizations would be helpful?

### Data Management
- How long should data be stored?
- Can users export their data?
- How is data organized or categorized?
- Can users search their data?
- What happens to deleted data?

## Business Context

### Stakeholders
- Who is the primary stakeholder or decision maker?
- Who else needs to approve this product?
- Who will be responsible for maintaining it?
- Who are the key team members involved?
- Who has final say on product decisions?

### Constraints
- Are there budget limitations?
- Are there timeline constraints?
- Are there technical limitations to be aware of?
- Are there regulatory or compliance requirements?
- Are there brand guidelines to follow?

### Integration
- Does this need to work with existing systems?
- What data needs to be shared between systems?
- Are there authentication requirements?
- What APIs or services need to be integrated?
- What happens if integrations fail?

### Business Model
- How will this product make money?
- Who pays for the product?
- What is the pricing structure?
- Are there different tiers or plans?
- What features are free vs. paid?

## Success & Metrics

### Success Definition
- What does success look like for this product?
- How will you measure success?
- What metrics matter most?
- What would make you consider this a failure?
- What milestones are you aiming for?

### User Metrics
- How many users do you expect?
- What percentage should be active users?
- How often should users return?
- What is an acceptable churn rate?
- What engagement level indicates success?

### Business Metrics
- What revenue targets do you have?
- What conversion rates are you aiming for?
- What is the target customer acquisition cost?
- What is the expected lifetime value?
- What ROI are you expecting?

### Quality Metrics
- What performance standards are required?
- What uptime is acceptable?
- What response time is expected?
- What error rate is tolerable?
- What satisfaction score are you targeting?

## Competition & Alternatives

### Current Solutions
- How do users currently solve this problem?
- What products or services already exist?
- What do users like about current solutions?
- What do users dislike about current solutions?
- Why isn't the current solution good enough?

### Competitive Analysis
- Who are your main competitors?
- What do they do well?
- What do they do poorly?
- How will you differentiate?
- What can you learn from them?

### Market Position
- Are you creating a new category or entering an existing one?
- Are you targeting underserved users?
- Are you competing on price, features, or experience?
- What is your unique advantage?
- Why will users switch to your product?

## Future Vision

### Roadmap
- What comes after the initial launch?
- What features are planned for version 2?
- How do you see this product evolving?
- What capabilities might be added later?
- What is the long-term vision?

### Scalability
- How many users do you expect to support?
- What happens as usage grows?
- What features become more important at scale?
- What challenges might arise with growth?
- How will the product adapt to more users?

### Expansion
- Could this product expand to new user types?
- Could this product expand to new use cases?
- Could this product expand to new markets?
- What adjacent problems could you solve?
- What would a product family look like?

## Risk & Challenges

### Potential Issues
- What could go wrong with this product?
- What assumptions are you making?
- What unknowns concern you?
- What dependencies worry you?
- What could cause this to fail?

### User Adoption
- What might prevent users from trying this?
- What might cause users to abandon it?
- What objections might users have?
- What friction exists in adoption?
- What would make users skeptical?

### Technical Challenges
- What technical challenges do you foresee?
- What are the hardest parts to build?
- What requires specialized expertise?
- What has the most uncertainty?
- What could cause delays?

## Clarification Questions

### When Requirements Are Vague
- Can you give me a specific example?
- What would that look like in practice?
- Can you walk me through a scenario?
- What would a user actually do?
- How would that work step by step?

### When Requirements Conflict
- How do you prioritize between [A] and [B]?
- If you had to choose, which is more important?
- Can both of these be true at the same time?
- What trade-offs are you willing to make?
- Which user need takes precedence?

### When Scope Is Unclear
- Is this a must-have or nice-to-have?
- Does this need to be in the first version?
- Could this be added later?
- How critical is this feature?
- What happens if we don't include this?

### When Technical Details Emerge
- Let's focus on what the user experiences - what should they see?
- From the user's perspective, what happens?
- What outcome are you looking for?
- What problem does this solve for users?
- How does this benefit the user?
